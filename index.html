How Holo-Battles Work

Rules, locations, and how a match plays out

The Basics

Format

3v3 turn-based. One active creature per side at a time. Knock out all 3 enemy creatures to win. Pets take no real damage — fully healed after every match.

Your Turn Options

Use ability — pick 1 of 5 moves (powerful ones have cooldowns)
Swap creature — switch to a teammate mid-battle
Surrender — forfeit

Turn Order

The creature with the highest Agility acts first. Creatures can randomly dodge, land crits, or get bonus moves.

Before the Match

Pick your 3 creatures and order before the fight. You can inspect the opponent's team first — plan your counters around what you see.

Battle Flow

Inspect the enemy team's affinities

Pick your 3 creatures and their starting order

Highest Agility creature goes first

Pick an ability — watch cooldowns on strong moves

Swap to a teammate if the matchup is bad

Last team standing wins

Where to Find Arenas

🌀 Space Anomaly

Main hub. Talk to Iteration: Oceanus for the tutorial and the daily challenge (same for every player worldwide). Also where PvP multiplayer happens.

🚀 Space Stations

Near mission and trade terminals. NPCs here are system champions — harder but give better nanite rewards.

🏘 Settlements & Trade Outposts

Seating areas near NPC clusters. Easier opponents, great for leveling new creatures. Their teams are built from local planet fauna.

🏗 Your Own Arena

Buildable at your base. Open it to other travellers, host tournaments, or challenge friends.

Daily tip: Oceanus resets every day. The puzzle is the same for all players — check the NMS community online if you're stuck.

Stats & Tiers

Understanding your creature's numbers and how to improve them

The 3 Stats

⚔ Combat Effectiveness

Raw damage output. Controls how hard offensive abilities hit — attacks, DoTs, combos. Prioritize this for an aggressive playstyle.

⚡ Agility

Does two things: determines who goes first each turn (highest goes first), and gives a chance for incoming attacks to miss. Fast creatures can dominate by acting first every round.

❤ Health

How much damage the creature can take before being knocked out. Larger creatures tend to have more HP but are easier to target. Good for tank-style play.

🧠 Personality & Size

Personality affects battle behavior (aggressive vs defensive decisions). Physical size affects durability and how easy the creature is to hit.

Tier System

S
Legendary. Best possible stats. Rare — found on uncommon biome planets. Worth investing all upgrade resources into.
A
Excellent. Strong stats. Fully competitive, especially with type advantages. Can beat S-tier with good strategy.
B
Solid. Good for most NPC fights. Will struggle against maxed S-tier opponents in PvP.
C
Beginner. Starting point. Upgradeable through XP mutations and Retroviral Pellets.
You can't skip tiers — complete one before the next unlocks. Decide which creature to invest in before spending Retroviral Pellets.

How to Upgrade

XP Mutations (Free)

Every battle gives XP. Accumulate enough to unlock mutations that boost: move-first chance, dodge rate, HP, and ability power.

Retroviral Pellets (Fast)

Directly boosts a specific stat. Dropped occasionally from victories. Summon the creature first before using one — otherwise it gets wasted.

Move Catalog

All move types from game data. Each creature gets 5 moves based on species & biome.

Basic Attacks
Basic AttackStandard non-typed hit. No elemental advantage or penalty.
Affinity AttackUses the creature's own affinity for a typed hit. Gets the full damage bonus against weak opponents.
Typed Attacks ×8One dedicated attack per affinity. Each uses full type matchup rules.
Wild SwingLower hit chance, but both a normal and affinity version exist. Can catch cautious players off guard.
Multi-Hit & Scaling
BarrageFires 3 affinity projectiles in one turn with escalating power (VeryLight → Light → Medium).
Ramp AttackGets stronger every time you use it in a match. Starts weak — becomes devastating in long fights.
Speed Strike ×2Scales with Agility. One version powers up with your speed; the other hits harder the faster the enemy is.
Multi-Turn AttackChannels over 3 turns with escalating damage (VeryLight → Medium → Heavy). Opponent can try to shield or interrupt.
Charge-Up Attacks
Chargeup StrikeSpends one turn charging, then delivers a strong hit. Telegraphed — opponent knows it's coming.
Double ChargeupWaits two full turns then fires a very heavy attack. Extremely telegraphed but potentially the hardest-hitting move in the game.
Damage Over Time & Traps
Affinity DoTApplies an elemental damage-over-time effect that ticks each turn for several rounds. Great vs. high-health tanks.
Bomb / Delay AttackPlants a timed debuff that explodes for big damage after X turns. Forces opponent to choose: dispel it or eat the hit.
DoT BombApplies sustained ticking damage AND a delayed burst in one move.
Don't Touch (Trap)Debuff that explodes if the enemy tries to dispel it. Hard counter to opponents running Cleanse moves.
Attack + DoT ComboDeals immediate damage AND applies a DoT in the same move. Both a normal and affinity version exist.
Heals & Shields
Self HealInstantly restores a portion of your creature's HP. Simple and reliable.
Heal Over Time (HoT)Regen effect that slowly heals over several turns. Better total healing than instant but spread out.
Delayed HealSets up a heal that triggers after X turns. Pre-plan recovery before taking incoming hits.
Shield ×2Creates a damage-absorbing barrier. Basic shield absorbs limited damage. Full Shield completely blocks all incoming hits until the limit is reached.
Reflect Shield ×2Sends damage back to the attacker. Normal version reflects a portion. Full Reflect bounces everything until the shield breaks.
Absorb ShieldFully absorbs all incoming hits until the damage limit is reached.
Self Buffs
Buff DamageIncreases your creature's damage output for a few turns. Stack with high Combat Effectiveness for big burst.
Buff AccuracyIncreases hit chance. Useful against high-Agility enemies who dodge frequently.
Buff Crit ChanceIncreases critical hit chance. Pair with high Combat Effectiveness for big burst potential.
Speed Up / Buff AgilityTwo versions: one boosts speed for next round; the other permanently raises Agility during the match.
Buff DodgeIncreases dodge chance. Combine with high Agility to make your creature very hard to hit.
BurrowGrants 100% dodge for one full round — cannot be hit. Best used to survive a telegraphed charge-up.
Reset CooldownsResets all your ability cooldowns instantly so you can reuse powerful moves right away.
Self DispelRemoves all debuffs from yourself. Counters DoTs, bombs, nerfs — but watch for Don't Touch traps.
Enemy Debuffs
Debuff DamageReduces the enemy's damage output. Very useful against high-attack S-tier opponents.
Debuff DefenseReduces enemy defense, making all your hits deal more damage. Stacks well with offensive teams.
Debuff AccuracyReduces enemy's chance to land attacks. Combines well with Agility buffs.
Combo DebuffReduces both accuracy AND damage in one move. Cripples offensive creatures efficiently.
PurgeStrips all buffs from the enemy. Counter to opponents stacking damage, crit, or speed boosts.
Change Affinity ⚡Forces the enemy to change their affinity type. Can completely flip a bad matchup into your favor. Very rare.
StunMakes the enemy skip their next turn entirely. Cancels active charge-ups.
Risky / Advanced
Self-Dot StrikeBig hit but applies a DoT to yourself. Use when you can KO the enemy next turn or you have healing support.
Attack BothDamages both the enemy AND your own creature. Good for finishing off a low-health enemy when time is running out.
Enrage ×3Massive hit that also buffs the enemy — one version gives +Crit, one +Damage, one +HoT. Trade burst for empowering your opponent.
Sacrifice ×2KOs your own creature to either buff your team's damage or shield the rest of your team. Turn a dying creature into an advantage.
ReviveBrings a knocked-out teammate back into the fight. Extremely rare. Can completely change a losing situation.
