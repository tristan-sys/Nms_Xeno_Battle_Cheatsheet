Battle Basics

How holo-battles actually work

The combat loop is simple once the structure clicks: read the lineup, lead the right creature, manage cooldowns, and swap before a bad matchup snowballs.

Format

Battles are 3v3 and turn-based. One active creature fights at a time. Knock out all three opposing creatures to win. Pets do not keep battle damage after the match.

Your options each turn

Use one of five moves, swap to a teammate, or surrender. Stronger moves usually come with cooldown windows, so the whole turn flow is about timing and pressure.

Turn order

Higher Agility acts first. Agility also helps attacks miss, so fast creatures can control tempo even before raw damage enters the picture.

Before the fight

You pick your three creatures and order before the match starts. Always inspect the enemy team first and lead into the matchup you actually want.

Battle flow

  1. 1Inspect the opposing affinities and likely role coverage.
  2. 2Choose your three creatures and decide which one should start.
  3. 3Use early turns to establish advantage with Agility, shields, pressure, or debuffs.
  4. 4Respect cooldown timing on both sides. Telegraphed charge-up turns are where defensive tools shine.
  5. 5Swap if the active matchup turns bad. Trading tempo is better than losing a creature for nothing.
  6. 6Close the fight by denying enemy recovery and punishing their long cooldown windows.

Where to fight

Space Anomaly: Oceanus handles the tutorial and daily challenge.
Space Stations: tougher champions and better nanite rewards.
Settlements / Trade Outposts: easier opponents for leveling and testing lineups.
Player arenas: best place to host or practice custom matches.

Affinities

Read the matchup first

Each creature's affinity comes from the planet biome it was found on. Affinity is the fastest way to tell whether a lead is safe, risky, or begging for a swap.

Before each fight, inspect the enemy team and deploy the creature that hits into their weakness. If the board turns, swap early rather than forcing a losing exchange.

Stats & Tiers

What the numbers are really doing

Damage is not the whole story. Agility determines who moves first, Health decides whether a creature can stay in the fight, and personality or size nudges how safe or punishing a build feels.

Combat Effectiveness

Raw offensive pressure. Most direct damage, DoT burst, and combo damage lean on this first.

Agility

Controls turn order and improves your chance to avoid incoming hits. It is both tempo and defense.

Health

How much room a creature has to survive burst, shields coming down, or a bad swap.

Personality and size

These shape feel more than raw math: aggression, defensive leaning, and how vulnerable the creature feels on board.

S
Legendary.

Best-in-class stats. Rare, worth long-term investment, and the easiest place to dump upgrade resources without regret.

A
Excellent.

Competitive with proper affinity coverage and move timing. Strong enough for almost every serious lineup.

B
Solid.

Very serviceable for standard NPC fights and decent testing, but it will feel the ceiling against optimized endgame teams.

C
Starter tier.

Good for growing, learning the systems, and deciding where your pellets and mutation progress should really go.
